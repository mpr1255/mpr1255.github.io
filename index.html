Work:

I am a doctoral candidate in political science at the Australian National University. I also do data science for the Victims of Communism Memorial Foundation based in Washington, DC, where I am involved in Xinjiang-related research. My work deals with biopolitics, state predation, and political violence, with a focus on organ trafficking. This work has led me to become something of a digital archivist, and I now maintain a number of large collections of PRC primary source materials, including court decisions, Party documents, and media and academic databases. I primarily use computational and quant methods but I am also very interested in theory.

I've previously worked as a legal researcher, human rights researcher, and an interpreter and translator for financial companies.

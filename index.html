Work:

I started at SGI in April 1996. I'm currently in the Java group. I'm also still spending some of my time working on STL extensions with Matt Austern and Alex Stepanov.

Before that, I was at Xerox PARC, where I worked on garbage collection, among other things. This work was started at Rice University, where it grew out of work on the implementation of the Russell programming language. It is being continued at a reduced level at SGI.

Prior to my stay at Rice, I was an undergraduate at the University of Washington, a grad student at Cornell University, and an Assistant Professor at the University of Washington.

Along the way I've also worked on various other subjects, including constructive real arithmetic, parallel attribute evaluation, decidability of type inference, and implementation of monitor locks without hardware support.
